Communication Trainer · Financial Model

The 10-year game, in three futures

A real-time AI communication coach (Deepgram streaming + LLM feedback, corpus behind an MCP server), sold freemium with a subscription core. Starting from zero distribution. Figures are USD; KRW shown at ₩1,350/$1. Net profit is after 22% Korea corporate tax with loss carryforward.

Read this before the numbers: two levers decide everything

This product is not classic SaaS. Every session burns AI cost-of-goods, and a free tier means you pay to serve users who never pay you (the freemium tax). The result the model keeps showing: the difference between the Realistic and Bull futures is not user count — it is a few points of paid-conversion and gross margin, compounded over ten years. Note the sobering line below: even in the Realistic case, with 2.2M users at year 10, cumulative 10-year net profit is still slightly negative. You spend the decade building the asset; the real money is year 11 onward.

The levers, ranked by leverage

  1. Paid conversion. Moving from 2.5% → 6% is most of the gap between a hobby and a $145M business. Your practice-and-feedback loop is the conversion engine — free users who feel themselves improving convert.
  2. Gross margin = AI cost control. Streaming STT + LLM feedback is your COGS. Hard free-tier caps, cheaper models, on-device voice activity detection. Every margin point is worth millions by Year 8.
  3. The freemium tax. At millions of free users, serving non-payers is a real line item. Cap free usage tightly or it eats the gross profit the payers generate.
  4. Distribution speed. Sets the top line, but note: scale without the three levers above just means losing money faster (see Bear vs Bull, same product).

On reading this: these are illustrative scenarios, not a forecast — a 10-year model is a structured argument about which levers matter, and its precision is false comfort. The honest headline is that distribution gets you users, but unit economics decide whether users are worth having. Starting from zero audience, your first real milestone is not revenue: it is proving the free-to-paid loop works on a few thousand people, because that single ratio is what separates all three futures above.
