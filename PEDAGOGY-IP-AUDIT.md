## Framework applied

1. **Copyright, idea/expression dichotomy (17 U.S.C. 102(b)).** Copyright protects a
   work's specific expression, not the ideas, methods, systems, procedures, or facts it
   describes. Communication techniques (open questions, discovery-before-pitch, story
   structure, pause for emphasis, follow-up questions) are unprotectable ideas/methods.
   Anyone may teach them.
2. **Derivative works (17 U.S.C. 101, 106(2)).** A close paraphrase that recasts a
   specific author's expression can be an infringing derivative work even if reworded.
   The mitigation is clean-room authoring from independent public sources.
3. **Clean-room / independent creation.** Independent creation is a complete defense to
   copyright infringement. The project's quarantine protocol (author from public canon
   and operator knowledge; never open the source during authoring) is a textbook
   clean-room posture and is the single strongest fact here.
4. **Trademark, nominative fair use.** Referring to a methodology or product by its name
   to describe it (SPIN, Sandler, "upfront contract") is generally permissible nominative
   use. Risk arises only if a third-party mark is used AS Cadence branding or to imply
   endorsement.
5. **Contract / terms of service (the non-copyright risk).** Paid courses frequently
   carry terms prohibiting use of the materials to build a competing product. This is a
   CONTRACT question independent of copyright, and clean-room does not cure a contractual
   breach if the operator agreed to such terms. This is the residual risk most worth
   checking with real counsel.
6. **Trade secret.** Low relevance: the techniques are publicly taught, not secret.
7. **Fair use of the private gap-check.** A one-time private, non-distributed read of
   purchased material to check completeness is a defensible transformative research use,
   but fair use is a defense, not a right. Keeping it private and never letting it seed
   product content is the correct posture.

## Findings

- The three specs contain only unprotectable ideas, methods, and facts, expressed in
  original language with original examples. On the copyright axis, infringement risk is
  **low**.
- Structure and ordering are grounded in independently citable public theory (learning
  science; named public methodologies), reinforcing independent creation.
- No trademarked course name is used as Cadence branding in these specs. Nominative
  references are acceptable.
- The corpus reviewed is specifications, not full unit content. Clearance of the
