# COMMUNICATION PRINCIPLES (our clean corpus, consolidated)

Compiled 2026-07-03 from SALES-PEDAGOGY.md, PEDAGOGY.md (public speaking), and
EVERYDAY-PEDAGOGY.md. All authored from public canon + first principles; none from the
quarantined courses. Tag: [chat] = transfers cleanly to a text/chat app; [voice] = needs
spoken delivery, does NOT work in pure chat. No em-dashes.

## Meta-principles (how we teach and score, applies to all modes)
- Content-couple every skill: a technique only counts if it lands on meaning (a pause on
  a key line, emphasis on a content word, a follow-up on their actual answer). Faking it
  requires doing the real thing.
- Band thresholds, not floors: overdoing a behavior fails as surely as underdoing it
  (too many questions = interrogation; too much emphasis = no emphasis).
- Evidence mandatory: every judgment quotes the transcript span that earned it.
- Coach, not certifier: make honest practice the path of least resistance.
- Learning science behind the ordering: isolate then integrate (cognitive load), scaffold
  foundations first, gate on mastery, deliberate practice with immediate specific feedback.

## SALES (consultative, discovery-led)
Foundations
- Sell from a service/diagnose frame, not a convince/win frame. [chat]
- Open with an upfront contract: set the agenda and possible outcomes, including "no". [chat]
- Listen more than you talk; reflect back. [chat]
Discovery
- Open questions produce information; feature talk produces resistance. [chat]
- Map the current situation before hunting for problems. [chat]
- Surface the real problem, not just the stated want. [chat]
- The first problem named is rarely the one that moves them; probe two or more levels to
  the real why (the motivation/emotion behind it). [chat]
- Make the cost of inaction explicit, ideally in the prospect's own words. [chat]
- People buy to resolve a felt consequence, not to fix a surface symptom. [chat]
Presentation
- Frame price against the cost of inaction, never in a vacuum. [chat]
- Trial-close to take temperature before the final ask. [chat]
Objections
- Objections are requests for information, not rejection. [chat]
- Find and isolate the real objection before answering any objection. [chat]
- Handle money by returning to value and cost of inaction. [chat]
- Separate genuine logistics from stalls; surface the real fear under a stall. [chat]
Closing
- Make a clear, direct, unambiguous ask. [chat]
- After the ask, hold silence and let them answer. [voice] (partially [chat])
- Never end without a concrete, scheduled next step. [chat]
Countable behaviors: talk 30-45% in discovery; 4+ open questions before any pitch; no
monologue over ~150 words; do not pitch before discovery.

## PUBLIC SPEAKING (delivery + narrative)
Foundations
- State the talk's single promise and honor it end to end. [chat]
- Regulate nerves with a pre-talk routine. [voice]
- Hold a grounded, still base; no fidget or pacing. [voice]
Voice (all [voice], do not transfer to chat)
- Vary pace deliberately: slow for weight, quick for energy.
- Project and vary volume for emphasis.
- Break monotone with pitch variation.
- A held pause before your most important line makes it land; silence is emphasis you do
  not have to earn with volume.
- Stress meaning-bearing words; match tone to content.
Body (all [voice])
- Stable stance; purposeful gestures that illustrate, not fidget; sustained eye contact
  with individuals, not scanning; movement that serves content, not pacing.
Narrative (mostly [chat])
- A story earns attention with a hook, holds it with a struggle, pays off with a
  resolution. [chat]
- Concrete specifics (named person/place, number, sensory detail) beat vague summary. [chat]
- Make the stakes legible: if X then Y. [chat]
- Open with a scene, tension, or claim, not an agenda or a throat-clear. [chat]
- Dynamic sentence rhythm: mix long sentences with short punch sentences. [chat]
- Active verbs over be-verbs. [chat]
- Rhetorical devices (tricolon, contrast, deliberate repetition) when they serve the
  point, not as decoration. [chat]
Countable: filler rate low; emphasis on 5-25% of words and on content words; 2+ specifics
per 60s of story.

## EVERYDAY (connection + conversation) [the chat app's core]
Foundations
- Show up warm, open, unhurried, with visible genuine interest. [chat]
- Start conversations naturally and low-pressure. [chat]
- Manage your own social anxiety so you are easy to talk to. [chat]
Curiosity
- Ask open questions, not yes/no. [chat]
- The follow-up question, not the first question, signals real listening and raises
  likability (question-asking research). [chat]
- Make it about them: go one level into what a fact meant to them. [chat]
- Blend questions with sharing so it never feels like an interrogation. [chat]
Listening
- Reflect or acknowledge what they said before you add your own. [chat]
- Build on what they said (yes-and); connect rather than reset. [chat]
- Read the room: notice interest, energy, disengagement, and adjust. [chat]
Being interesting
- Tell concrete, vivid, short stories instead of vague summaries. [chat]
- Reciprocate: share something real, then call back to them. [chat]
- People do not remember the interesting person; they remember the person who made THEM
  feel interesting. [chat]
Depth
- Move gracefully from small talk to something that matters. [chat]
Core mechanic: connection comes from making people feel heard, not from impressing them;
warmth is earned by genuine reciprocity, and flattery alone does nothing.
Balance: roughly 50/50 talk; spotlight 40-70% on them; never run 3+ consecutive questions
with zero self-disclosure (interview mode fails).

## Cross-cutting (shared signals across all three)
- Talk less, listen more (talk/listen ratio).
- Open questions over closed.
- Follow-ups that reference the prior answer (depth).
- Concrete specifics over vague generality.
- Story spine: setup, turn, resolution.
- Control fillers.
- Do not monologue.

## For the chat app
The EVERYDAY pack transfers whole. Sales discovery/objection LOGIC transfers (question
quality, isolating objections, real-why probing); its live-pressure closing bits are
weaker in text. Public-speaking VOICE and BODY do not transfer, but the NARRATIVE set
(hook, struggle, resolution, specifics, stakes, punchy rhythm) transfers and powers the
"tell a great short story / be interesting" skill.
