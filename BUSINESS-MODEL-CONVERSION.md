# Business model and conversion

How the charisma app makes money and why a user upgrades. Companion to
AVATAR-TIER-PRICING.md (the numbers) and PRD-CHARISMA-CHAT.md (the product). No em-dashes.

## The model in one line
Free text (habit + proof) -> 15-minute avatar trial (taste the real thing) -> paid avatar,
$14.99/month, 120-minute cap.

## The free/paid split (decided)
- **Text chat: fully free**, abuse-limited only (PRD section 10), no monetization cap.
- **Avatar (live, voice, lip-synced): the paywall.** One paywall, and it is the avatar.
- **No middle text paywall.** A "$6.99 unlimited text" tier muddies the story and competes
  with the avatar. Avoid it.

Reasoning: unlimited text has weak willingness-to-pay (low marginal value, and users doubt
that being good at texting transfers to real life). Live voice-avatar practice has strong
willingness-to-pay: it is visibly the real thing, it plausibly transfers, and it carries the
emotional payoff. So text is the hook, the avatar is the product you sell.

## Why a user converts (the psychology)
Users do not pay for more of the free thing. They pay when they hit the wall the free thing
cannot cross: the skill they actually want is talking to real people, out loud, with timing
and presence, and you cannot train that by typing. Text teaches and MEASURES the concepts
(open questions, follow-ups, reciprocity) and proves "charisma is a trainable, measurable
skill." The avatar is where the skill becomes real. That gap is the paywall.

## The four conversion moments (build these)
1. **Right after a win.** They just scored well in text and feel it. Offer the trial on
   confidence, not frustration: "you have the fundamentals, try it live."
2. **The taste, then the loss.** 15 minutes talking to a real face is the aha; when it ends,
   typing feels flat. Loss aversion sells.
3. **A concrete upcoming event.** Date, interview, networking night, hard conversation.
   Willingness-to-pay spikes with a real thing on the calendar: "rehearse it live before
   Friday."
4. **The plateau.** Text score maxes out and the product names its own ceiling: "the next
   level is live conversation."

## What keeps them paying (retention, not just conversion)
The streak and rising score (investment), new characters and scenarios, felt real-world
results ("I actually did better at X"), and rehearsing specific upcoming conversations (the
stickiest, highest-value use). Conversion gets the card; felt improvement keeps it.

## Trial economics (watch this number)
- 15-minute trial x ~$0.03/min ~= $0.45 per trial-taker, converters and non-converters alike.
- Needed conversion: roughly 10 to 15%+ trial-to-paid, or acquisition underwaters. At 15%,
  ~$3 per acquired subscriber against a $14.99 product, which is healthy.
- Instrument trial-start, trial-complete, and trial-to-paid from day one. If conversion sits
  below ~10%, the problem is either the trial placement (offer it after a win, not cold) or
  the free text not building enough investment first.

## The risks
- **Text has to be genuinely good.** It is the top of the funnel; a thin demo leaks users
  before they reach the trial.
- **Avatar cost is variable.** The funnel math must clear the conversion threshold above;
  this is the one number that can sink the tier.
- **Transformation has to be real, not just an in-app score.** If users do not actually get
  better at real conversations, retention and word of mouth die regardless of the funnel. The
  pedagogy must target real transformation (see the research plan), and the app should measure
  real-world improvement, not only in-app metrics.
