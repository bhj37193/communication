# Go-deeper library: talks and research, mapped to skills

Reference-only and clean-room: each entry is our own summary of a public talk or study,
used to deepen a skill. Nothing here is served to the model or quoted in-product.

## everyday-connection / yes-and

- Celeste Headlee, "10 ways to have a better conversation" (TED). Enter every
  conversation assuming you have something to learn; if you don't know, say so; do not
  equate your experience with theirs (their loss is not your opening to talk about
  yours). Maps to: follow-ups over prepared lines, the anti-monologue rules.
- Huang, Yeomans, Brooks, Minson, Gino, "It doesn't hurt to ask" (J. Personality and
  Social Psychology, 2017). Follow-up questions specifically, not question volume,
  raise likability; speed-daters who asked more follow-ups got more second dates. Maps
  to: the followups signal being the hard gate, open_questions being capped.
- Arthur Aron et al., the closeness-generation study (the "36 questions"). What works
  is escalating MUTUAL self-disclosure, both people, in turns. Using the list as a
  question bank is the exact failure mode the north star warns about. Maps to:
  reciprocity, interview_mode.

## open-questions

- Lera Boroditsky, "How language shapes the way we think" (TED). The frame of a
  question changes the answer you get; wording is not neutral. Maps to: open vs closed
  starters, question quality over question count.

## reciprocity

- Brené Brown, "The power of vulnerability" (TED). Being seen is the price of
  connection; armor reads as distance. Maps to: the reciprocity signal, why questions
  alone keep warmth capped.

## reading-the-room

- Pamela Meyer, "How to spot a liar" (TED). Incongruence between words and behavior is
  readable; attention to the other person's signals is trainable. Maps to: noticing a
  cooling thread, adjusting instead of pushing.

## storytelling

- Nancy Duarte, "The secret structure of great talks" (TED). The shape that holds
  attention: what is, what could be, struggle before resolution. Maps to: the short
  story spine (hook, struggle, payoff), specifics over summary.

## presence [voice]

- Julian Treasure, "How to speak so that people want to listen" (TED). Pace, pause,
  register, and the listening that earns the right to be heard. Voice-only; parked
  until a voice mode exists.
