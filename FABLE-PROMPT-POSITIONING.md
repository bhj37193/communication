# FABLE PROMPT: positioning for the communication practice app

Paste everything below into a fresh session. It is self-contained.

---

You are a sharp consumer-product positioning and brand strategist (think category
design, April Dunford style). This brief is self-contained; reason independently and
decide. Do not hedge into a menu; make a call, defend it, and name the strongest
objection to your own pick. No em-dashes in your answer.

## The product
A consumer mobile/web app that makes people better communicators through daily practice.
Core loop: open the app, get ONE short daily challenge, a text-chat scenario with an AI
character (for example: "match with someone giving you dry one-word replies and warm them
up", or "your coworker just got back from a trip, make them feel interesting"). You chat
it out in text, the AI character reacts realistically (warms up if you reciprocate, drifts
if you interrogate or make it about yourself), and you get instant, specific feedback: one
thing you did well (with your actual line quoted) and one thing to fix. Progress and a
streak advance. 3 to 5 minutes.

Under the hood: a real pedagogy engine (mastery-based skill progression, deterministic
scoring of concrete signals like question quality, follow-ups, talk balance, reciprocity),
so progress is measured, not vibes. It is text/chat, not voice. It runs on a cheap LLM, so
cost is roughly a penny per session, which makes a free daily challenge economically
viable (freemium).

## Decided context
- Consumer, chat-based, freemium, daily-habit product.
- Leaning toward the everyday/social/dating communication lane (highest willingness to pay
  and virality), but the exact beachhead is OPEN.
- The pedagogy/mastery engine and a scoring validator already exist and are reusable.

## The competitive field (for contrast, this is a positioning problem)
- Rizz and similar: AI "do it for me" reply-generators for dating/texting. ~$7/week, viral,
  bootstrapped to real revenue. They hand you the line in the moment.
- Speak: AI conversation-practice app for LANGUAGE learning. $1B valuation, ~$100M ARR.
  Practice by talking, positioned against Duolingo's "vocab and rules."
- Duolingo: daily-habit language learning; the gamification/streak/freemium gold standard.
- Character.AI / Replika: AI companions/roleplay; extreme engagement (60+ min/day) via
  characters you connect with; some skew young and have faced safety scrutiny.
- Yoodli and B2B tools: AI communication/sales coaching, more professional/enterprise.
- ChatGPT voice/text: the free generic baseline anyone can improvise with.

## Founder context (weigh, do not ignore)
Solo founder, limited budget, cannot fund paid acquisition, so growth must be organic or
viral. On an F-1 visa (revenue authorization pending), so early moves are intent-capture.
The founder's background edge is sales, though this is now a consumer play.

## What I need you to decide
1. CATEGORY: what category is this in, or should it create? ("communication practice", a
   "conversation gym", "social fitness", "dating confidence trainer", something else?) Is
   category creation worth it here or a trap?
2. BEACHHEAD: the single sharpest audience to launch to (a specific person with a specific
   pain), and why that one over the alternatives (dating/texting confidence, general adult
   social skills, professional/work communication, the socially anxious, etc.).
3. CORE PROMISE: the one-sentence value proposition. What transformation, for whom.
4. DIFFERENTIATION: how it is positioned against the named competitors, especially the
   "do it for me" reply-generators (Rizz) and the generic baseline (ChatGPT). What is the
   defensible wedge in one line.
5. TAGLINE / MESSAGING: 3 to 5 candidate taglines or headline messages, and your pick.
6. NAME DIRECTION: the qualities the product name should have (not necessarily a specific
   name), and whether "Cadence" (the current placeholder, which collides with SalesLoft
   Cadence) should be kept or dropped.
7. TRAPS: the positioning mistakes most likely to sink this (for example, sounding like
   therapy, sounding like a dating cheat tool, sounding like homework, being confused with
   a companion/roleplay app, being too broad).

## How to answer
Give a clear recommendation for each of the 7. Then write a single crisp positioning
statement in the form: "For [beachhead] who [pain], [product] is a [category] that [core
benefit], unlike [main alternative], because [wedge]." Finally, state the single strongest
argument against your recommended beachhead and what evidence would make you switch. Decide.
