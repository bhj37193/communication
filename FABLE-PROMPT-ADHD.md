## The concept
A proactive AI "body double" for adults with ADHD. On a schedule (like cron), it calls the
user (voice), and instead of just reminding them, it starts the routine or task WITH them,
one step at a time, staying present and accountable while they do it, something they can
narrate to and perform "for." Setup is by voice (you brain-dump your routines by talking;
the AI structures them into simple step-by-step SOPs), so it requires no ongoing discipline
to maintain. One-line framing: "a body double that actually shows up and gets you started."

## The problem (from real Reddit research on r/ADHD and r/adhdwomen)
Adults with ADHD cannot START tasks they know they need to do, and they abandon every
reminder/to-do/routine app. The ROOT CAUSE is not memory, it is an emotional barrier the
community calls the "Wall of Awful": anticipation inflates the task ("picturing all the
steps makes it seem more daunting than it should be"), and a shame spiral makes it worse
("you avoid it, feel worse, avoid it more"). Reminder apps fail because they poke at memory,
not the emotional wall, and users ignore the notification anyway. Verbatim: "task initiation
problem EVEN AFTER notifications from Tiimo and Structured."

## What demonstrably works (also from the research)
Body doubling: doing a task in the presence of an accountable other. Overwhelming evidence:
"Body Doubling is Magic," "the only reason I got through college was studying WITH friends,"
"a person doing activities WITH me, this works." Key nuances:
- The presence does NOT have to be human: people already body-double with a robot vacuum
  ("turning on the roomba initiates my task").
- A voice/narration variant works: "I do it out loud like I'm making an instructional
  video," "pretending I'm doing it for other people helps."
- The deepest tell: ADHD people act for an external witness they cannot summon for
  themselves ("I do this exact thing for my job, just not for myself").

## Competitors
Focusmate and body-doubling websites (HUMAN, scheduled, requires booking a session with a
stranger). Tiimo, Structured (notification/routine apps that users say fail at initiation).
Medication (helps, but shortage, side effects, and many explicitly want non-med tools).
Siri/Reminders/generic AI assistants.

## Founder constraints
Solo, small budget, must build largely with AI, needs something buildable now. Bar: users
pay ~$10/mo and there are ~1,000 reachable paying users. Voice calls carry real COGS
(~$0.10-0.30/call, so a daily caller could cost $3-9/mo).

6. RETENTION RISK: ADHD users are notorious for loving a new tool then abandoning it

7. Sharpest beachhead WITHIN ADHD: which sub-group and which tasks/routines to launch on?
8. The single biggest reason this fails, and the CHEAPEST test to de-risk the make-or-break
   assumption before building anything.
9. VERDICT: does this clear "solvable today, $10/mo, ~1k reachable users, defensible enough
